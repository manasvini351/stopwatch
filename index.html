Why do you need a Stopwatch?

In any race such as swimming or track a stop watch can provide split or lap times to determine an athleteâ€™s performance during an event. By adjusting tempo and movement swimmers or runners can adjust their pace and breathing to insure they have enough energy in reserve for the end of a distance race. The difference between a gold or silver medal for the 400 m freestyle during the 2016 Rio Olympics was 3:41.55 to 3:41.68. Of course with advanced timing technology, Olympic athletes can be measured to one-millionth of a second, or a microsecond -- (note that it takes 300 to 400 microseconds for an eye to blink). Digital stop watches can record 1/100th of a second whereas an analog chronograph watch. (a watch with a stop watch feature), can measure 1/10th of a second.

Stopwatch can measure a time interval up to 0.01 second.
